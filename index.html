How it works

A judgment layer over the raw signal.

Each claim arrives as plain-language signal. The console runs it through the Lifetime Warranty Plan rubric in fixed order, auto-decides the clear cases with the clause cited, and escalates the rest with a handover note.

Scope: the Lifetime Warranty Plan only. Cars24's 30-day and 12-month tiers are out of scope by design.

What's covered

  • EngineCylinder head, block, crankshaft, rods, pistons, rings, camshaft, valves, oil/water/vacuum pumps. Seals excluded.
  • TransmissionManual & automatic: gears, shafts, selectors, synchromesh hubs, planetary sets. (MT / AT / AMT / CVT)
  • DrivetrainPropeller shaft, universal joints, front & rear differentials.

What voids or excludes

  • Voids allMissed service (6mo/6,000km), 12yr / 1,50,000km cap, commercial use, ownership transfer, misuse, tampering.
  • ExcludesWear-and-tear (clutch plates, belts, tyres), cosmetic, accident/flood, non-powertrain electronics, consumables.

Decision order · strict sequence

1

Void check first

Any void condition? Deny outright, cite it. A voided warranty makes coverage moot, so this runs before everything.

2

Coverage

Failed part outside the three covered systems? Deny, outside coverage scope.

3

Exclusion

Covered system, but failure stems from an excluded cause (wear, flood)? Deny, cite the exclusion.

4

Approve

Passes all three cleanly? Auto-approve, zero human touch.

5

Escalate

Cause genuinely ambiguous: can't separate covered-defect from excluded-cause? Hand to a human with a note.

What's next

The real prize: warranty economics.

Because the agent reads every approved claim, it's the natural place to cost-tag each refurb and weigh it against the fees collected — turning the warranty book from a black-box liability into a measured P&L.

One claiming car costs more than its own fees — so the ratio that matters is a portfolio number, measured live across the book, not reconstructed after the quarter closes.

Not a saving the agent invents — a human rejects a bad claim too. It's a capability the agent unlocks by sitting on every decision.

Ground it in the car's record

Pull the car's real service log, plan tier and odometer so the agent checks actual coverage — not form fields. The C-42 and C-43 escalations exist only because it can't yet see the source record.

Scale across the book

The rubric is component-and-clause based, not model-specific — the same logic runs across hundreds of models and thousands of claims, clearing the ~80% and cost-tagging as it goes.
